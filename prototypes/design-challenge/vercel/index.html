This is a known issue with multi-state payroll processing after tax registration updates. Here are the recommended steps:

Direct Deposit Issues

This guide covers common direct deposit failures in QBO, including ACH processing delays, bank verification issues, and state-specific routing problems.

Always verify the customer's bank prenote status before troubleshooting deposit timing issues.

Common Causes

  1. Stale ACH cache: After tax registration updates, the ACH batch processor may not recognize new state tax IDs for up to 24 hours. See INV-482901.
  2. Bank prenote rejection: If the employee's bank rejected the prenote, deposits will silently fail. Check Payroll Settings > Direct Deposit for prenote status.
  3. Multi-state routing: Employees in 3+ states may trigger a routing table lookup that times out. This is a known backend issue.
  4. Holiday processing: ACH does not process on federal holidays. Deposits submitted Friday after 5PM EST won't initiate until Monday.

Resolution Steps

  1. Navigate to Payroll > Payroll History and confirm the run status shows "Processed"
  2. Check Payroll Settings > State Tax Setup for all affected states
  3. Verify prenote status under Banking > Direct Deposit Settings
  4. If ACH cache is suspected, advise customer to wait 24h and re-submit
  5. For urgent cases: cut manual checks as interim, void after DD processes
If the customer has been waiting 3+ business days, the transactions were likely never initiated. Escalate to INV.

Related INVs

  • INV-482901 — Multi-state DD failure after tax registration update (47 affected)
  • INV-482865 — TD Canada Trust duplicate transaction feed
